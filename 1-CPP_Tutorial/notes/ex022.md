# C++ Data Types

## Exercise 022

In C++, a variable must hold a specific data type. The available types are:

- `bool` — 1 byte — stores `true` or `false` values.
- `char` — 1 byte — stores a single character, letter, or number.
- `int` — 2 or 4 bytes — stores whole numbers.
- `float` — 4 bytes — stores fractional numbers containing one or more decimal places.
Sufficient to store 6 to 7 decimal digits.
- `double` — 8 bytes — also stores fractional numbers, but holds more decimal digits than
`float`. Sufficient to store 15 decimal digits.
