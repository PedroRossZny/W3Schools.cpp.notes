# C++ Numeric Types

## Exercise 023

Use `int` for whole numbers such as 35 or 1000, and `float` or `double` for floating
point numbers such as 9.99 or 3.14515. `double` has greater precision than `float`, with
around fifteen digits compared to `float`'s seven, so `double` is preferred for most
calculations. A floating point number can also be a scientific number, using the letter
`e` to indicate a power of 10, as shown in **ex023**.
