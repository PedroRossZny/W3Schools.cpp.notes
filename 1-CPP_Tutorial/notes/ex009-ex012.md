# C++ Variables

Variables are containers that store data values. There are different types of variables,
for example:

- `int` — stores whole numbers, such as 123 or -123.
- `double` — stores floating point numbers with decimals, such as 19.99 or -19.99.
- `char` — stores individual characters, such as 'a' or 'B'. They are delimited by single
quotes.
- `string` — stores text, such as "Hello World". They are delimited by double quotes.
- `bool` — stores values with two states, `true` or `false`.
