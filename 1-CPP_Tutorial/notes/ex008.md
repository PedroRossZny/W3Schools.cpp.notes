# C++ Comments

## Exercise 008

Comments can be used to explain code, making it more readable. We can also use them to
prevent a statement from being executed by turning it into a comment, since comments are
not executed and are only visible to whoever is writing the code.

Comments are divided into two types:

- **Single-line comments** `//` — place the slashes at the beginning of a line to comment
out the entire line. We can also add them at the end of a line of code, allowing us to
run a statement and show a comment in the same place.
- **Multi-line comments** `/* */` — open with `/*` and close with `*/`, allowing comments
to span multiple lines since we control where they end.

In general, `//` is used for short comments and `/* */` for longer ones, as shown in
**ex008**.
